THE PROBLEM

SAR TEAMS ARE OUTGUNNED
BY TERRAIN.

Every minute a subject is missing, the search radius grows. Ground teams are limited by darkness, canopy, and communication gaps. A single drone helps — but it's still just one set of eyes covering a fraction of the ground.

01
Coverage Bottleneck
A single drone covers 1–2 square miles per hour in dense terrain. With a 72-hour survival window shrinking fast, that math doesn't work. Swarm technology changes the equation — five thermal sensors covering parallel grid sectors simultaneously, multiplying coverage without multiplying pilots.
02
Nighttime Blindness
Over 40% of SAR activations happen at night or in zero-visibility conditions. Human searchers are nearly useless in dense canopy after dark. Most commercial drones lack the thermal resolution to separate a human heat signature from ambient ground warmth at operational altitude.
03
Comms Collapse
Remote terrain kills radio range. Cell towers don't exist in the canyons, forests, and river corridors where people get lost. When communications fail, ground team coordination collapses — and the search falls apart precisely when every second counts.
THE SYSTEM

OSPREY-5 ECOSYSTEM

A purpose-built three-tier aerial platform — mothership, leader, and four autonomous followers — engineered specifically for the realities of search and rescue operations in communication-denied, low-visibility environments.

01
Helium Mothership
A high-altitude tethered aerostat that launches first, maps the full search area from elevation, and acts as a persistent communications relay — keeping the swarm online even when cell towers and ground radios are out, using Meshtastic mesh protocol across the entire operational area.
02
Leader Drone
The only drone a human directly pilots. Loaded with LiDAR, dual-band thermal imaging, and live mapping. It leads the swarm, processes anomaly data, and maintains formation — freeing the operator to focus entirely on the search rather than managing individual aircraft.
03
Four Follower Drones
Fly autonomously in a V-pattern behind the leader, each carrying a thermal camera. When any follower detects an anomaly, it breaks formation and hovers to confirm — then pushes GPS coordinates to the ground station and ATAK with no human input required.
04
ATAK / CoTAK Integration
All drone positions, thermal contacts, and confirmed anomalies overlay onto the same TAK common operating picture used by ground teams and incident command. Every responder sees the full aerial picture — and no ground searcher gets mistaken for the missing subject.
OPERATIONS FLOW

FROM CALLOUT TO CONTACT

Engineered for rapid deployment — from Pelican case to active aerial sweep in under 15 minutes, with no specialized infrastructure required on scene.

I
ARRIVE & ASSESS
Team arrives on scene. Incident command defines the search perimeter and inputs it into the ground control station. The entire Osprey-5 kit deploys from a single portable case — ready to launch in minutes regardless of terrain or infrastructure.
T+0 MIN
II
MOTHERSHIP ASCENDS
The helium aerostat launches first, climbing to operational altitude above canopy. It begins wide-area thermal and optical mapping while simultaneously establishing a Meshtastic mesh communication node that restores team connectivity across the entire search zone.
T+8 MIN
III
SWARM DEPLOYS
The pilot launches the leader and the four followers auto-form into V-pattern. The swarm begins its systematic grid sweep — up to 50 square miles per deployment — with parallel thermal coverage across all five sensors covering the search area simultaneously.
T+12 MIN
IV
CONFIRM & VECTOR
On anomaly detection, a follower breaks formation and hovers for visual confirmation. Coordinates transmit to ATAK instantly. Ground teams vector toward the contact while the remaining swarm continues sweeping adjacent sectors without any interruption to coverage.

MISSION PROFILES

WHERE OSPREY-5 OPERATES

Engineered for the full spectrum of emergency response — from major disaster events down to a single missing hiker after dark in terrain with no comms.

DISASTER RESPONSE
When everything fails at once
Earthquakes, wildfires, and major storm events destroy roads, down power lines, and collapse of communication simultaneously. The Osprey-5 mothership re-establishes a comms relay in minutes while the swarm maps structural damage and flags survivor heat signatures across a massive impact zone.
  • Rapid wide-area damage assessment
  • Comms relay when all towers are down
  • Works alongside Red Cross, Team Rubicon, FEMA
  • Live thermal and optical feed to incident command
AVALANCHE SAR
Racing the burial clock
Avalanche survival drops sharply after 15 minutes of burial. Ground probe lines are slow. Beacon searches are limited to one point at a time. The Osprey-5 swarm blankets a debris field with thermal — detecting residual body heat through compacted snow faster than any ground team can cover the same terrain.
  • Full debris field thermal sweep in minutes
  • Precise GPS burial coordinates to rescue teams
  • Operates in high-altitude, extreme cold conditions
  • Complements beacon search — does not replace it
WILDERNESS SAR
Lost in the backcountry
Dense tree canopy, no cell coverage, zero visibility after dark. The mothership climbs above the canopy to map while the swarm sweeps below the treeline with thermal — detecting body heat through foliage even when a subject is unconscious and cannot signal their location.
  • Hill country, river corridors, dense cedar
  • Active night sweeps with thermal IR
  • Mesh comms when ground radios fail
  • Compatible with TEXSAR and Travis County SAR protocols
FLOOD & SWIFT WATER
When the water rises fast
Flash floods strand people on rooftops, in vehicles, and on high ground before boats can safely reach them. The swarm surveys a 50-square-mile inundation zone in a single flight — flagging thermal signatures while waterways are still too dangerous for any surface rescue vessel to operate.
  • Survivor detection on rooftops and high ground
  • Safe to fly while conditions are dangerous for boats
  • Continuous overhead coverage during active rescue
  • Live feed to swift water teams and incident command
URBAN MISSING
Dementia, mental health, children
Urban and suburban missing persons cases demand massive coordination across neighborhoods, parks, and drainage systems. The swarm provides overhead thermal coverage that multiplies the search team's effectiveness — covering in one pass what takes dozens of volunteers hours on foot in the dark.
  • Nighttime perimeter sweeps
  • River, drainage channel, and park coverage
  • ATAK integration with all ground teams
  • Significantly reduces false-positive callouts
